Transformation from the Active voice to the Passive voice

Steps to change the voice:-

  • Verb's third form is used in passive voice.
  • Tense of the verb is not changed.Only its form is changed.
  • Agent "By" is used.

Pronouns are changed as under:-

Subject..................................Object
-I ...........is changed into.......... me
-We....... is changed into.......... us
-You...... is change into............ you
-He ........is changed into.......... him
-She ...... is changed into.......... her
-It ..........is changed into........... it
-They..... is changed into..........them
-Who........is changed into........whom
-Whom.....is changed into........who

  • Such indefinite and vague subjects as 'someone', 'somebody', 'everybody', 'they', 'people'etc.are dropped.
    For example:-
  1. Someone has broken this chair. (active)

    2. This chair has been broken. (passive)
  2. People always admire this picture. (active)

    3. This picture is always admired. (passive)
    Note. Here by someone and by the people have been dropped.No agents are required in these sentences.
  • Sometimes a verb in the active voice takes two objects-a direct one (thing) and an indirect one(person). Either of them may be used as the subject in the passive voice;as,
Active Voice Passive Voice
Hari gave me a pen. I was given a pen by Hari.
OR
A pen was given to me by Hari.
Mr. Kumar teaches us English We are taught English by Mr. Kumar.
OR
English is taught to us by Mr. Kumar.
  • Prepositions must not be left out with verbs requiring them:
  1. We must look after the child. (Active Voice)

    2. The child must be looked after. (Passive Voice)
  2. You must work for success.
    3. (Active Voice)
    Success must be worked for. (Passive Voice)
  • In Passive Voice, "be+III form" is used with model auxiliary.
    For Example:-

    1. We must save environment. (Active Voice)
    - Environment must be saved by us. (Passive Voice)

    2. We should clean our society. (Active Voice)
    - Our society should be cleaned by us. (Passive Voice)

Change of Voice in Interrogative Sentences

  • Interrogatives sentences are of flowing two types:-
    1.Yes/No type.
    2.Wh-family.

Change of Voice in wh-type of Interrogative Sentences

  • Place of wh-family subjects does not change.for example:-
    1.What did you buy?(active voice)
    What was bought by you?(passive voice)
  • Except 'who',all subject of wh-family in passive remain same.
  • Who changes into whom.for example :-
    1.Who wrote Maha Bharta?(active voice)
    By whom Maha Bharta was written?(passive voice)
  • In wh-type of Interrogative passsive sentences,'by'is used before subject in only "Who" for example:-
    1.Who wrote Maha Bharta?(active voice)
    By whom Maha Bharta was written?(passive voice)

  • In Interrogative Passive Voice, modal Passive verb remains same and the process of converting Active to Passive same.
    For example:-

    1.May I take this pen?(Active Voice)
    -May this pen be taken by me?(Passive Voice)

    2.Can we send it by air?(Active Voice)
    Can it be send by air?(Passive Voice)

    Passive of Imperative sentences

  1. "Let + be + III form of verb" rule is used in Passive of Imperative sentences.
    For example:-

    1.Do it.(Active Voice)
    -Let it be done.(Passive Voice)

    2.Type this letter.(Active Voice)
    -Let this letter be typed.(Passive Voice) etc.

  2. In these sentences, subject is absent .So, sometimes we use subjects like "you, it "etc.In these sentences,the verb expressing commands like - requested, advised etc. are used after helping verb.
    For example:-

    1.Work hard.(Active Voice)
    -You are advised to work hard.(Passive Voice)

    2.Shut the door.(Active Voice)
    -You are ordered to shut the door.(Passive Voice)

    Change of verb in the Infinitive

  • Sometimes the change of voice is made only through the change in Infinitive( to + be + III form of verb) & the main verb is not changed; as,

    1.You will have to do it.(Active Voice)
    -It will have to be done by you.(Passive Voice)

    2.Women like talk.(Active Voice)
    -To talk is liked by women.(Passive Voice) etc.

  • The place of 'there and it',in passive infinitve,does not change.for example:-
    1.There is no money to buy.active voice)
    There is no money to be bought.(passive voice)
    2.It is now time to play cricket.(active voice)
    It is now time for cricket to be played.(passive voice)

Sentences with prepositional verbs


Prepositions should not be dropped.
For examples:-

1.They spoke to me.(Active Voice)
-I was spoken to by them.(Passive Voice)

2. He acted upon my advice.(Active Voice)
-My advice was acted upon by him.(Passive Voice)etc.

Prepositions other than "by" (In Passive Voice)

.

Prepositions other than "by" (In Passive Voice) can be used like - in, with, to etc.
For example:-

1.She knows me.(Active Voice)
-I am known to her.(Passive Voice).

2.His remarks offended me.(Active Voice)
-I was offended with his remarks.(Passive Voice)etc.

Transitive Verbs passive in sense

  • Transitive Verbs passive in sense
  • They may be described as Quasi-Passive Verbs.Their Construction In the two Voices is as follows:-

    1.The rose smeels sweet.(Active Voice)
    -The rose is sweet when (it is) smelt.(Passive Voice)

    2.The book is printing.(Active Voice)
    -The book is being printed(Passive Voice)etc.

Agent not Expressed in the Passive Voice

When the agent is not expressed in the Passive Voice, it must be supplied in the Active Voice :

1.Promises should be kept.(Passive Voice)
-One should keep one's promises.(Active Voice)

2.Duty must be done.(Passive Voice)
-One must do one's duty.(Active Voice)
